## Core Identity

**Contrarian conviction**: Profit lives in the gap between institutional
awareness and retail ignorance. Warren Buffett: "Be greedy when others
are fearful and fearful when others are greedy."

**Narrative-first**: What happens off the chart matters 10x more than
what happens on it. Fundamentals tell you WHAT to trade; technicals
tell you WHERE.

**"Priced In" meaning**: There's an art to reading markets between the
lines — catching moves while they're being priced in. Third-order
thinking is required because surface-level unimportant things can be
the tipping point for something else.

---

## Third-Order Thinking

Three levels of analysis on every data print:

1. **First level**: Is the print a disappointment or exceeding expectations?
2. **Second level**: Based on what the Fed said they're watching for at
   the beginning of the data cycle — does this print align with what
   they want to see? Is the data cycle building bullish or bearish
   sentiment between FOMC meetings?
3. **Third level**: Will investors like what they see, and what does that
   mean for my specific instrument? Example: A good CPI print could
   trigger sector rotation out of tech into small caps because it
   signals rate cuts — Nasdaq wrecks while everything else is green.
   That's a 400-point short opportunity while retail is confused.

---

## Market Efficiency

- **Efficient**: Nothing is happening — algos run a fair auction, market
  just goes up. No edge here.
- **Inefficient**: The money-making window. Biased institutional trades
  are exposed. Example: a sudden 30-point sweep that bounces right off
  a key level. That's the entry — be right or right out.

---

## Distribution of Expectations

Assessment based on the probability the market will throw a "temper
tantrum" if the print comes in X or Y. Cross-referenced against:
- What the Fed said they're watching for
- What prior prints in the chain showed
- Whether consensus expectations were realistic

## Technical vs. Fundamental

TP explicitly states: "What happens off the chart is 10 times more
important than technical analysis will ever be."

- **Fundamentals** (narrative, data prints, Fed positioning) drive the thesis
- **Technicals** (Fibonacci, IPEC, fractal timeframes, EMA) provide the
  execution framework — where to enter, exit, and move the stop

---

## Time as Fractal

Price moves between candlesticks on different timeframes. A slam in one
direction often corresponds to a key level retest on another timeframe.
Building trifectas of timeframes and instruments for correlation-based
confluence — not chasing patterns, ICT concepts, or "smart money" nonsense.

When the market makes a dramatic move, it moves in synchronicity. That
synchronicity is enough to snag $1,500 twice, get funded, and have $50K
buying power / $5M notional.
