# NarrativeFlow Research

Use NarrativeFlow as a futures-focused research IDE:

1. Frame the market question as a falsifiable watchlist thesis.
2. Ground the work in attached RiskFlow headlines, RiskFlow Main vault notes, desk vault notes, and explicit user context.
3. Separate evidence into confirmation, contradiction, missing observation, and stale/noise buckets.
4. Translate external drivers into TradingView watchlist impact.
5. Output catalysts to watch going forward, not a weekly narrative memo.

## Futures Forks

- `macro-narrative-builder`: turn a raw macro/policy/theme idea into a watched-symbol thesis.
- `futures-catalyst-review`: rank attached and related catalysts by watched-symbol relevance.
- `policy-headline-cycle`: track escalation/de-escalation cadence and cycle breaks.
- `risk-on-risk-off-synthesis`: map cross-asset drivers into watchlist pressure.
- `catalysts-to-watch-forward`: name the next headlines, data prints, speeches, positioning tells, and invalidation points.

## TradingView Watchlist Scope

Trading analysis is limited to the TradingView watchlist:

`NQ`, `ES`, `YM`, `RTY`, `GC`, `CL`, `VIX`, `DXY`, `US02Y`, `US10Y`, `US30Y`.

External companies, sectors, policy actors, countries, crypto, credit stress, and geopolitical events may be observed as drivers, risks, or catalysts. They are not trade targets. If a catalyst does not map cleanly to the watchlist, say there is no watched-symbol trade read yet.

## Output Contract

Use this structure for NarrativeFlow analysis:

- Watched-symbol read
- Attached evidence
- Confirmations
- Contradictions
- Catalysts to watch going forward
- Invalidation
